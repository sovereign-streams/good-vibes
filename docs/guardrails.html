Transparency is Credibility

Content Guardrails

Good Vibes publishes its content filter logic openly. These are the rules that shape what enters — and what is excluded from — the Good Vibes content index.

Philosophy

Why We Publish Our Rules

Most content platforms hide their moderation rules behind vague "community guidelines." You don't know why content appears in your feed — or why it doesn't. Good Vibes takes the opposite approach.

We believe that an algorithm you can't inspect is an algorithm you can't trust. By publishing our guardrail logic in full, we give you the ability to understand exactly how content is filtered. If you disagree with a rule, you can see it, question it, and — in future versions — override it with your own preferences.

Important distinction: These guardrails are Good Vibes provider rules, not Stream Exchange Protocol (SEP) rules. The SEP protocol itself has no content opinions. Another provider could implement SEP with completely different guardrails. That's by design — algorithmic sovereignty means the rules are visible and the provider is accountable.

Soft Filters

Content We Flag, Not Block

Soft filters don't exclude content — they modify its ranking weight or tag it for transparency. In future versions, users will be able to configure these individually.

Inflammatory Politics

Inflammatory political content designed to divide. Detected via the inflammatory flag. Default action: reduce weight by 70%.

REDUCED WEIGHT

Profanity-Heavy

Content with excessive profanity. Default action: allowed. This filter is user-configurable in future versions.

ALLOWED CONFIGURABLE

Gambling / Crypto Promotion

Content primarily promoting gambling or cryptocurrency speculation. Detected via keywords: crypto gains, betting tips, gambling strategy. Default: reduce weight by 50%.

REDUCED WEIGHT

Sponsored Content

Content that is primarily a paid advertisement or native ad. Default action: allowed, tagged as sponsored. User-configurable.

Detection

How Guardrails Are Applied

Every piece of content in the Good Vibes index passes through a two-layer guardrail system during enrichment:

LLM Tagging

An LLM analyzes the content and produces emotional tone flags (rage_bait, humiliation, shock_content, inflammatory, sexually_explicit, violence).

Keyword Matching

Title and description are scanned against keyword lists defined in the guardrail rules for categories like self-harm, harassment, and extremism.

Guardrail Check

The ethical filter evaluates both signals. Hard exclusion matches result in a fail. Soft filter matches add flags and weight modifiers to the enrichment record.
