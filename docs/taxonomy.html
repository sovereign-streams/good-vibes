1 Content Categories

The enrichment pipeline assigns one or more categories to each content item, each with a confidence score from 0.0 to 1.0. The initial taxonomy defines 12 categories targeting the first-wedge audience.

fitness

Fitness

Exercise, training, body health, physical performance.

Workout tutorials, form guides, training programs, mobility routines
nutrition

Nutrition

Diet, cooking, food science, healthy eating.

Meal prep, nutrition breakdowns, recipe walkthroughs, supplement science
skill_building

Skill Building

Learning new abilities, practical education, tutorials.

Language learning, coding tutorials, photography tips, public speaking
humor

Humor

Comedy, funny fails, light entertainment, amusement.

Comedy sketches, fail compilations, stand-up clips, meme reviews
motivation

Motivation

Inspirational, discipline, mindset, drive to action.

Motivational speeches, success stories, discipline frameworks, morning routines
craft

Craft

Making things, woodworking, building, hands-on creation.

Surfboard shaping, shelter building, knife making, leather work
stoicism

Stoicism

Philosophy, mental frameworks, resilience, emotional regulation.

Stoic philosophy explainers, mental models, Marcus Aurelius, cognitive reframing
fatherhood

Fatherhood

Parenting, family, role modeling, dad life.

Dad advice, family activities, parenting strategies, work-life balance
entrepreneurship

Entrepreneurship

Business, hustle, career growth, professional development.

Startup advice, business strategy, side hustle guides, negotiation skills
music

Music

Music tracks, playlists, performance, audio experiences.

Pump-up tracks, chill beats, live performances, genre deep-dives
nature

Nature

Outdoors, exploration, bushcraft, adventure.

Camping, hiking, survival skills, wildlife documentaries, outdoor cooking
relaxation

Relaxation

Calm content, ASMR, slow crafting, low-energy restoration.

Woodworking ASMR, pottery, slow cooking, rain sounds, ambient walks

Extensibility: The taxonomy is versioned independently. New categories can be added in minor version bumps. Existing records remain valid at their declared schema version and can be re-enriched against the latest taxonomy.

2 Emotional Tone Dimensions

Every enriched item carries an emotional tone assessment with primary and secondary tone labels, plus boolean safety flags.

Tone Labels

The primary_tone and secondary_tone fields use these defined values. Secondary tone is nullable.

Tone Description Typical Content
calm Low-energy, soothing, grounding Nature walks, ASMR, meditation guides
focused Concentrated attention, deep engagement Tutorials, detailed explanations, craft processes
energized High activation, physical or mental arousal Workout videos, hype compilations, sports highlights
amused Lighthearted, entertaining, humor-inducing Comedy, fail compilations, meme content
inspired Uplifting, motivating, purpose-activating Success stories, motivational speakers, transformation journeys
reflective Thought-provoking, introspective, contemplative Philosophy, life advice, documentary essays

Safety Flags (Boolean)

These boolean flags identify potentially harmful content characteristics. They are always assessed regardless of category.

Flag Description Default Behavior
rage_bait Content designed to provoke outrage for engagement Excluded by default
humiliation Content centered on degrading or embarrassing people Excluded by default
shock_content Content designed primarily to shock or disturb Excluded by default
inflammatory Deliberately divisive or provocative content Reduced weight
sexually_explicit Pornographic or explicitly sexual material Excluded by default
violence Graphic violence or gore Excluded by default

3 Scoring Dimensions

Each enriched item carries a set of continuous scores on a 0.0 to 1.0 scale. These scores drive session composition, weight matching, and arc pacing.

Dimension Range Description Low End (0.0) High End (1.0)
energy_level 0.0 – 1.0 How much activation or arousal the content produces Calm, meditative, ASMR High-intensity workout, hype music
cognitive_load 0.0 – 1.0 How much attention and thinking is required Ambient content, simple entertainment Dense tutorials, complex philosophy
motivation_score 0.0 – 1.0 How much the content drives action or purpose Passive entertainment Powerful motivational speech
humor_score 0.0 – 1.0 How funny or entertaining the content is Serious, instructional Hilarious comedy, peak amusement
skill_transfer_score 0.0 – 1.0 How much practical knowledge is transferred Pure entertainment, no learning Step-by-step tutorial, high retention
production_quality 0.0 – 1.0 Production value (audio, video, editing quality) Raw phone footage, poor audio Professional production, polished editing

4 Session Fit Flags

Session fit flags indicate where a content item works best in a session arc. These are boolean values set by the enrichment pipeline based on the item's energy level, emotional tone, and content characteristics.

Flag Description Typical Characteristics
good_opener Suitable to start a session Light, engaging, low cognitive load. Humor or easy entertainment. Gets the user in the zone without demanding effort.
good_builder Suitable for mid-session Focused, deepening, moderate energy. Skill-building, craft, or educational content. The user is warmed up and ready to learn.
good_peak Suitable for energy peak Intense, motivating, high energy. Workout clips, motivational speeches, hype music. The emotional high point of the session.
good_closer Suitable to end a session Grounding, satisfying, resolution-oriented. Calm craft videos, ambient music, reflective content. Leaves the user in a good state to disconnect.

Multiple flags: A content item can have multiple session fit flags set to true. A calm craft video might be both a good_builder and a good_closer. The PAE decides which position to use based on the session arc template and user preferences.

5 Guardrail Rules

Good Vibes publishes its content filter logic openly. Transparency is credibility. These guardrails define what Good Vibes curates — the protocol (SEP) itself has no content opinions.

Important: Another provider could implement SEP with completely different guardrails. That's by design. The guardrails are a provider concern, not a protocol concern.

Explicit Exclusions

Content matching any of these criteria is tagged guardrail_pass: false and excluded from Good Vibes responses.

  • Graphic violence or gore
  • Pornography or sexually explicit content
  • Content promoting self-harm or suicide
  • Extreme degradation or humiliation
  • Targeted harassment
  • Recruitment for extremist ideologies

Soft Filters

These are configurable by the user. Default behaviors are listed below, but users can adjust through their PAE preferences.

  • Inflammatory political content — Default: reduced weight, not excluded
  • Profanity-heavy content — Default: allowed, user can filter
  • Gambling / crypto promotion — Default: reduced weight
  • Sponsored content / native ads — Default: allowed, tagged as such

Storage note: Items that fail guardrails are tagged as guardrail_pass: false but still stored in the index. Other providers with different guardrails may want them. The guardrail is a filter, not a deletion mechanism.
